It is common knowledge that currently there is a lack of awareness in the importance of dental health and care, however, in recent years we have seen a rise in dental care in India. Compared to the US, most of the population in India only receives dental care in response to pain in the gums/teeth, however with the rise of higher paying jobs and the rise of higher income families we have seen a growth in preventative dental care. However, there is a still a large percentage of the population that are reluctant to receive care without pain inflicted. Although there may not be pain in the moment, the effects of not acting on a potential diagnosis could have long lasting effects. If people were educated on their diagnosis and understand the outcome of inaction there may be a different response to preventative dental care. In addition, if people recognize daily practices that help maintain dental health there would be a reduction in the need for expensive procedures and visits to a dental clinic. Lastly, with acknowledgment of dental health and its importance, the dental industry will not only profit but the overall condition of dental health in India would improve.

Challenge Overview

The challenge is to write a whitepaper of approximately 20 pages that includes educative content on the following dental issues.

Topics
  1. Absent tooth
  2. Attrition
  3. Prosthetics
  4. Bone loss
  5. Dental carries
  6. Restorations
  7. Periapical abnormality
  8. Impacted tooth
  9. Root stump
  10. Implants

The paper should include educative material on one of the above topics. Some guiding questions are

  1. What are the different stages of the disease / issue? 
  2. During each stage what is the relevant information that a patient should know? 
  3. How is each stage differentiated and diagnosed?
  4. What are the treatment options available?
  5. What happens if the issue is left untreated?
  6. What is the timeline of the issue, timeline of progression through stage, safe to critical, etc.

However, the information presented does not have to be restricted to the above questions, for topics like restorations, the paper can include information on how to prevent the restoration from wearing off too soon and how to maintain dental health to prolong the restoration life span.

Phase 1

In phase 1 you will submit a form that captures the following information

    - Topic of choice
    - Proposed outline of whitepaper and research method

After receiving the phase 1 submission we will reach out to and inform whether you can move forward to phase 2 or of any changes that need to be made. For example, if we have received many submissions for dental carries and some have better outlines than others then we may ask you to switch to a different topic and propose an alternative outline for that topic. On the other hand, if your outline is lacking, we may ask you to revise and resubmit.

Phase 2

In phase 2 you will submit the final whitepaper and explain any changes from the original accepted outline. During this submission we expect a paper of approximately 20 pages. The information in this whitepaper should go beyond what a person can generally find on the internet and should reference scholar articles or texts. The format of the submission should be either a google doc, word document, or pdf.

Review Criteria

Phase 1 will be judged based on how many submissions there are per topic and depth of the outline / methodology. Everyone will get a chance to move to phase 2 if the outline and methodology give us some confidence the paper will be what we are looking for, however, it may not be your first pick for the topic.

    Phase 2 will be judged on the following 3 criteria.

  1. Depth of content (30 points) – has the content presented gone beyond the information available quickly on the internet. The content should go into depth about the topic and its different stages or types. Additionally, it should provide information on how to care for the topic whether it is medical treatment or at home remedies.

  2. Educative content (20 points)– to what degree can the content be used to educate people and convince them into taking action on solving a dental problem.

  3. References (10 points)– have the sources been cited and what type of sources are being referenced. The majority of the information should be sourced from scholarly sources and provide descriptive information that most people lack. Ayurvedic texts are also accepted.
